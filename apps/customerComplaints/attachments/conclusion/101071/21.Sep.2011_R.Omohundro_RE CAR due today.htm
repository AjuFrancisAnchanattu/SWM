I was able to review the panels that you sent, and showed them to our Technical department, but since I haven’t been able to recreate the issue with our retain samples so far.  I applied several lots of retain material to aluminum on 9/1/11 (at room temperature) and they still display no issues.  As a result of input from our technical department I have placed some of the samples in an oven to try to create simulated extended aging, but don’t have any results yet.

 

From discussion of the issue that shows in the panel samples you provided with Technical, one potential root cause that they identified was the degree of cure of the material.  However, since material can continue to cure after the production process there isn’t really a way to definitively determine if the material was under cured, since it now doesn’t display the issue in any retain samples.

 

As a result of the fact that I can’t confirm a definitive root cause, the only actions that we can implement would be actions to seek to increase the curing of the material during the process, and increasing the frequency of testing that may catch under curing of the material.  Additionally, I’ll be adding notes to the work instructions to have engineering and QA present the next time that this material is coated, to further investigate potential issues during the coating process with this issue.
